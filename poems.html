By Sunny Kay my gift

they say you need thick skin
to survive in this world
but maybe I am not a tree
and do not grow bark for protection
perhaps I am a flower
and that is my gift to humanity

By Sunny Kay they only share the letter "e" nothing else

I see the way he treats you
And saw as you withdrew

I hear the words he says to you
And know that you will only “solider through”

If this is the thing that you call love
Than I would rather be disposed of

For if love causes all the pain on your face
Then I would rather my ashes be placed in a vase

If love is staying quiet
As he begins a riot

That only leads to heartbreak
Than I would rather not partake

This thing you call love
Seems to be too much to take care of

The glimmer in your eyes has long since faded
And the strength in your back has become rather jaded

This love
You speak of

Seems like a long term illness
That kills without asking any forgiveness

I believe the word love, you have begun to confuse
And the word you are searching for is abuse

By Sunny Kay and I was crazy

There is an elephant over there
Oh! You don’t see it
Silly me! It’s behind you,
so you’ll have to turn your head

Oh… why would you do that?
I just thought we should acknowledge it
Maybe chat about why it’s here
How it came to be where we now are

I mean it is rather peculiar
Our doors aren’t big enough
And there is no way it came through the window
It wouldn’t fit at all

Do you think it was brought in as a calf?
Kept in secret all these years?
But, How did we not hear it?
How did we not see it?

Why aren’t you talking?
Oh, I’m a fool?
You say…

For acknowledging
The great big elephant
Staring at me in the living room.
